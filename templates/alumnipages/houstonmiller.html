I attended Maryville College from 2007 until 2011. I got to be the first person to graduate with a full Computer Science degree, as prior to that it was either CS/Math or CS/Business degree. I was lucky that I could focus purely on the computer science part since I was way better at that than higher level math and had no interest in business.

Maryville College helped me get an internship with Y-12 National Security Complex in Oak Ridge, TN and that led directly to me getting my first job with Y-12. I have since moved on from there, but nearly every job I have had since then has been because of the lasting relationships with friends I made while at Maryville College. MC also did a great job preparing me for public speaking, which is something I never thought I would do. The things I learned about speaking in front of an audience, thanks to my undergrad thesis and numerous other oral presentations for other classes, are still of use to me as I have spoken at several developer conferences, CodeStock, Devmoot, and DevSpace specifically. I definitely wouldn't be where I am today without the people and education I got while at MC.

Advice I have for incoming CS students would be to join the programming team! I am not just shilling for it, the programming team played a major part in building the bonds with the friends I have today and helped to sharpen my skills as a developer. Don't be afraid to check out different languages than the ones you learn in class and that StackOverflow is a website you should get really familiar with (professional developers use it every day!) Most of all, have fun with coding!
