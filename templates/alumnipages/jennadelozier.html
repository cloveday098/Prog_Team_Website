I am currently a Research Software Engineer at Oak Ridge National Laboratory. Here, I work on a variety of projects, mostly on the software development side. After my time at Maryville College, I have pursued MS in Computer Science at Georgia Tech, and I will be starting a PhD in Data Science & Engineering at the University of Tennessee in Fall 2024.

My education at Maryville College has helped me in many ways. From the liberal arts curriculum to the technical skills I gained through the Math & CS programs, I was well prepared for all the demands of my current position.

As a mathematics major with a minor in computer science at Maryville College, I found the education enriching, and it prepared me well for my current position. I know it’s probably not what a lot of STEM students want to hear, but the most important part of my education there was the well-rounded, liberal arts curriculum. Communication and critical thinking are two very important skills to have, even in a highly technical environment. Additionally, being able to adapt to different environments and learn new technologies is critical when working in STEM. So, I would summarize that as “learning how to learn” – I think this is by far the most important thing students can pick up in college.
